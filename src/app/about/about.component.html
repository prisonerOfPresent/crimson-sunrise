Etymology of the name

Arun(noun) Vishnu(noun)

Both the nouns Arun and Vishnu have their origins rooted in ancient Hindu mythology. Aruna devan is usually a reference to the Sarathi (charioteer) of Surya devan, the Sun-Deity. It is said that Aruna devan spreads light with a reddish hue, just before the actual sunrise - known as Arunodayam.

For the second noun, as the name suggests, Vishnu refers to the God Vishnu of the Holy Trinity, responsible for existence of reality.
It is also my Father's name.

Coming back to the first noun, Aruna varnam literally means the colour Red in Sanskrit and its derivatives. I usually think of this interpretation of the name, rather than the mythical - because that involves deities and I am not one, obviously :)

I prefer the The Crimson shade of Red, specifically.
